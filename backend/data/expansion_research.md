## Key Findings — Validated mechanism claims (each becomes a reusable tag/rule)

1. **CBD is a clinically significant CYP inhibitor.** CBD potently inhibits CYP3A4 and CYP2C19 in human liver microsomes and also inhibits CYP2C9, raising levels of co-administered substrates including warfarin, some statins, and antiepileptics (clobazam, valproate). *Source: Patsalos et al., Epilepsia 2020 (Epidiolex DDI review). Evidence grade C (mechanism + human PK).*

2. **GLP-1 agonists delay gastric emptying and can impair oral drug absorption.** Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) carries an FDA-label requirement that oral-contraceptive users switch to a non-oral method or add a barrier method **for 4 weeks after initiation and for 4 weeks after each dose escalation**; the Mounjaro label (§8.3) states verbatim: *"Use of MOUNJARO may reduce the efficacy of oral hormonal contraceptives due to delayed gastric emptying."* Injectable semaglutide labels state the drug *"causes a delay of gastric emptying, and thereby has the potential to impact the absorption of … oral medications."* Oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) label: *"Levothyroxine exposure was increased 33% (90% CI: 125-142) when administered with RYBELSUS in a drug interaction study"* (Hjerpsted et al., 45 healthy subjects, 600 mcg levothyroxine; baseline-corrected total-T4 AUC rose 33% with Cmax unchanged). *Source: FDA DailyMed labels. Evidence grade A/B. NOTE: the 4-week backup-contraception requirement is UNIQUE to tirzepatide — semaglutide products do NOT carry it.*

3. **Opioid + benzodiazepine carries an FDA boxed warning.** *"Concomitant use of benzodiazepines and opioids may result in profound sedation, respiratory depression, coma, and death."* Per the FDA news release of Aug 31, 2016, boxed warnings were required across **"nearly 400 products in total"** (opioid analgesics, opioid cough products, benzodiazepines). *Source: FDA. Evidence grade A.*

4. **Red yeast rice is pharmacologically a statin** — it contains monacolin K, which NCCIH states is *"structurally identical to the medicine lovastatin."* Per Cohen, Avula & Khan, *Eur J Prev Cardiol* 2017 (vol 24(13):1431–1434), of 28 brands from GNC, Walgreens, Walmart and Whole Foods, monacolin K was undetectable in 2 brands and in the other 26 *"ranged more than 60-fold, from 0.09 to 5.48 mg per 1,200 mg of red yeast rice"* (≈120-fold by daily serving) — so consumers cannot know their statin dose. *Source: NCCIH; Cohen 2017. Evidence grade A.*

5. **MAOI + sympathomimetic/serotonergic = contraindicated** (hypertensive crisis / serotonin syndrome). FDA MAOI labels state sympathomimetic drugs such as stimulants are contraindicated. *Source: FDA MAOI labels; StatPearls (NBK557395). Evidence grade A.*

6. **Tizanidine + strong CYP1A2 inhibitor = contraindicated.** Per the FDA Zanaflex label (study in 10 healthy subjects): fluvoxamine increased tizanidine Cmax 12-fold, **AUC 33-fold**, and half-life 3-fold; ciprofloxacin increased Cmax 7-fold and **AUC 10-fold** — causing severe hypotension, bradycardia, and sedation. Both combinations are contraindicated. *Source: FDA Zanaflex label. Evidence grade A.*

7. **Sildenafil + nitrates = absolute contraindication** (life-threatening hypotension; both elevate cGMP → profound vasodilation). The FDA sildenafil label lists nitrate/riociguat use as a contraindication. *Source: FDA DailyMed. Evidence grade A.*

8. **St. John's Wort induces CYP3A4** → reduces efficacy of oral contraceptives, testosterone/estrogen, and warfarin; and is serotonergic → serotonin syndrome with SSRIs/SNRIs. A controlled study (Hall et al., *Clin Pharmacol Ther* 2003, PMID 14663455) found St. John's Wort induced ethinyl-estradiol/norethindrone metabolism, causing breakthrough bleeding and recommending an added barrier method. *Source: NCCIH Herb-Drug Interactions; Hall 2003. Evidence grade B.*

### Additional supplement claims (cited)
- **Berberine** lowers blood glucose via AMPK activation (adds to diabetes meds and GLP-1s → hypoglycemia) and inhibits CYP3A4, CYP2D6, CYP2C9 (IC50 ~45 µM for CYP2D6) and P-glycoprotein. A meta-analysis (Front Pharmacol 2022) found reductions in fasting glucose and HbA1c. *Evidence grade B.*
- **Kava and green tea extract are hepatotoxic.** NCCIH notes kava is linked to rare but sometimes fatal liver injury; NIH **LiverTox assigns green tea extract a likelihood score of A** (well-established cause of clinically apparent liver injury, driven by EGCG). High-dose niacin (>1000 mg) is also hepatotoxic. *Evidence grade A/B.*
- **Biotin interferes with lab immunoassays.** Per the FDA Safety Communication (Nov 5, 2019, updating Nov 28, 2017): *"The FDA has received a report that one patient taking high levels of biotin died following falsely low troponin test results when a troponin test known to have biotin interference was used."* Also distorts thyroid and hCG tests. *Evidence grade A.*
- **Warfarin + supplements:** strong evidence that **ginkgo** increases bleeding risk; evidence for ginseng, fish oil, and garlic is conflicting (several RCTs negative). Vitamin K antagonizes warfarin. *Source: Dietary supplements and bleeding, PMC9586694. Evidence grade B.*
- **Antiepileptics deplete folate and vitamin D.** Linnebank et al. (*Ann Neurol* 2011, n=2730 AED-treated) found carbamazepine, gabapentin, oxcarbazepine, phenytoin, primidone, and valproate associated with lower folate; phenytoin causes vitamin D depletion/hypocalcemia. *Evidence grade B.*
- **Smoking induces CYP1A2**, lowering levels of caffeine, clozapine, olanzapine, and duloxetine. Per Psychiatric Times, *"smoking cessation would probably cause an average patient's blood clozapine level to increase by 1.5-fold two to four weeks later"*; induction reverses with an apparent half-life of ~39 hours (Faber & Fuhr, *Clin Pharmacol Ther* 2004). *Evidence grade A.*
- **Estrogen/testosterone + smoking** synergistically raise venous thromboembolism (VTE) risk; oral conjugated equine estrogen carries the highest VTE risk among oral preparations; the FDA issued a testosterone VTE warning in 2014. *Source: Pomp et al.; QResearch/CPRD nested case-control (PMC6326068). Evidence grade B.*

## Highest-Impact NEW Interactions to Feature in the Demo
These are confirmed by primary sources and are the ones an executive is most likely to surface by typing a common drug brand name + a Vitamin Shoppe product:

1. **Zoloft (sertraline) + St. John's Wort** → serotonin syndrome (major). *Stacks via serotonergic additive rule.*
2. **Ozempic/Mounjaro + any oral medication or birth control** → delayed gastric emptying; **tirzepatide carries an FDA-label 4-week backup-contraception requirement**. *Directly relevant — The Vitamin Shoppe sells GLP-1 nutritional support.*
3. **Xanax (alprazolam) + OxyContin/Percocet (oxycodone)** → FDA boxed warning, respiratory depression. *CNS-depressant stack escalates with alcohol/muscle relaxants/sleep aids.*
4. **Viagra (sildenafil) + nitrates** → absolute contraindication; **+ yohimbine or high-dose L-arginine** → additive hypotension. *Relevant to men's-health/sports-nutrition catalog.*
5. **CBD + warfarin (Coumadin) / statins / valproate** → CYP3A4/2C9/2C19 inhibition raises drug levels. *CBD is a major wellness-category product.*
6. **Red yeast rice + a statin (Lipitor/Crestor)** → duplicate HMG-CoA reductase inhibition (monacolin K = lovastatin). *RYR is a Vitamin Shoppe cholesterol-support product.*
7. **Adderall/Vyvanse + pre-workout (caffeine/synephrine/yohimbine)** → additive sympathomimetic cardiovascular load. *Sports-nutrition crossover.*
8. **Tizanidine (Zanaflex) + ciprofloxacin/fluvoxamine** → contraindicated (33-fold AUC rise → severe hypotension).
9. **Berberine + metformin / GLP-1** → additive hypoglycemia. *Berberine is a top metabolic/sugar-control seller.*
10. **St. John's Wort + birth control / TRT** → CYP3A4 induction → contraceptive failure / lower hormone efficacy.

## Recommendations
1. **Lead the demo with interactions #1–6 above** — each is primary-sourced (NCCIH/FDA/peer-reviewed) and maps to a real Vitamin Shoppe catalog product, making the "we caught something your shelf could cause" story land with executives.
2. **Add the 6 new mechanism tags to the engine vocabulary first**, then load Table 1 entities — they will immediately inherit the existing combinatorial additive rules without writing pairwise rows. This is where the tag-based architecture pays off most.
3. **Stage the JSON merge in this order**: (a) ENTITIES (so foreign-key slugs exist), (b) INTERACTION_PAIRS, (c) ADDITIVE_RULES, (d) CONDITIONAL_MODIFIERS, (e) SURVEY_RULES. Validate that every `agent_a`/`agent_b` and `target_entity` resolves to an `entity_id`.
4. **Treat severity escalation as the engine's job, not the data's**: the additive rules carry `escalation_map`; do not hard-code "contraindicated" in pairwise rows unless `is_true_contraindication=true` (only sildenafil+nitrate, tizanidine+CYP1A2 inhibitor, MAOI+stimulant, MAOI+serotonergic qualify here).
5. **Benchmarks that would change these rules**: if NCCIH/FDA upgrades injectable-GLP-1 absorption from "potential" to a labeled dose-adjustment requirement (currently only tirzepatide+contraceptives and oral semaglutide+levothyroxine are label-level), promote `glp1_oral_absorption` from moderate to major. If a future RCT confirms clinically significant CBD+statin interaction in humans (currently mechanism-grade C), upgrade `cbd_statin` to grade B/major.
6. **Scope note for future expansion**: MDMA/illicit stimulants + serotonergics or MAOIs are genuine, severe serotonin-syndrome/hypertensive-crisis risks, but are intentionally EXCLUDED from this wellness-retailer dataset. Flag for a future clinical-grade version only.

## Caveats
- **Evidence-grade honesty**: Several high-visibility supplement-CYP claims (CBD as CYP3A4/2C9/2C19 inhibitor; berberine as CYP3A4/2D6 inhibitor) rest substantially on in vitro/human-microsome and mechanistic data rather than large RCTs — graded C–D accordingly. The CBD+valproate hepatic signal and CBD+clobazam are the exceptions (grade A, from Epidiolex RCTs).
- **GLP-1 absorption effects are mostly labeled as "potential/theoretical" for injectables.** Only two interactions reach label-level certainty: tirzepatide + oral contraceptives (the only GLP-1 with a contraceptive-switch mandate) and oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) + levothyroxine (+33% AUC). Do not over-state injectable semaglutide absorption effects in the UI.
- **Warfarin + supplement evidence is genuinely conflicting**: RCTs are negative for ginseng, fish oil, and garlic; only ginkgo (and chondroitin-glucosamine) has strong coadministration evidence. The dataset reflects this — do not flag every supplement as a warfarin bleeding risk or you will produce alert fatigue.
- **Licorice nuance**: the potassium-lowering/hypertensive effect is from glycyrrhizin; **DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice)**, common in digestive products, lacks this and should not trigger the rule — the entity note captures this distinction.
- **Source tiering for citations**: where a consumer-blog snippet appeared in search, the dataset cites the underlying primary source (FDA label, NCCIH, PMC/PubMed) instead. A coding agent should preserve the `source_ref` URLs as the authoritative citation, not secondary aggregators.
- A few `source_ref` values point to a DailyMed search root rather than a fixed setid (e.g., levothyroxine) because DailyMed reassigns setids across label revisions; the coding agent should pin the current setid at merge time.
